ASCC During the COVID-19 Closure

The Academic Success and Career Center will continue providing services to students and alumni through a variety of online means. Until further notice, here is how we can help provide you with the same services as always during this difficult time. For up-to-date information on WSU’s policies and guidelines, please refer to the COVID-19 WSU page.

ASCC Support

  • Career Coaching and Assessments

    The Academic Success and Career Center is switching it’s drop-in meetings to be entirely online in support of social distancing guidelines. You will now schedule an appointment via Handshake for a future time and complete a Zoom appointment. With the screen sharing technology present in the video conferencing software Zoom we can review your application documents and navigate resources together in real time so that we can achieve the same level of collaboration that we could in person.

    Schedule Career Coaching Appointment

  • Academic Advising

    Our advisors’ goal is your academic success and can help you with classes, pass/fail options, changing majors and looking at other options. Academic Advising will still be set up through Navigate, although your actual meeting will take place via Zoom, your advisor will send you the information.

    Schedule Academic Advising Appointment

  • Drop-In Meetings for Academic Advising

    We still offer our drop-in hours of 2:00 pm to 4:00 pm, students can still speak with an Academic Advisor by joining the Zoom Meeting located below.

     

    Drop-In Appointments 2:00 to 4:00

  • Tutoring

    Our tutoring services have also moved online, you can find an available tutor to speak with below by entering in your subject you need help with. Meetings will be conducted via Zoom.

    Find a Tutor

  • Academic Success Coaching

    Our Academic Success Coaches are still available during our normally scheduled drop-in hours from 2:00 pm to 4:00 pm. Select a link from the bios below to speak with Edgardo or Jennifer today!

    Speak With an Academic Success Coach

    The CSF Kitchen and Personal Essentials Pantry is available to College Success Program scholars by appointment only to keep in line with Governor Inslee’s ‘Stay Home, Stay Healthy’ order. Director, Maria de Jesus Dixon, will schedule a pick-up time with you.

    Email for an Appointment

  • Cougar Closet

    The Cougar Closet is available to all WSU students by appointment only to keep in line with Governor Inslee’s ‘Stay Home, Stay Healthy’ order. Director of College Success Programs, Maria de Jesus Dixon, will schedule a pick-up time with you.

    Email for an Appointment

  • Wellbeing Online

    Wellbeing Online is dedicated to helping you develop skills that foster a happy, balanced life by taking a holistic approach to healthy living. We provide opportunities to learn about all 8 dimensions of wellbeing to help you identify multiple ways to improve your personal wellbeing.

  • Internet Capabilities

    Attending online classes are important. We have heard from a few students that they are dealing with weak internet capabilities and are having difficulty attending classes and Zoom meetings. If you are in this situation, there are several companies that are offering discounts or free service to students affected by COVID-19. This previously linked article may be helpful.

    We suggest checking with your local internet service providers to see what offers they are making available to students. Here are some that we found:

    • Spectrum – Offering Free internet for 60 days to college students
    • Centurylink – Suspending data usage limits for current customers
    • Sparklight – Offering unlimited data on all internet services
    • Verizon – Automatically appyling 15GB of high speed data for wireless customers from Mar 25 – Apr 30
