Discover career opportunities in Europe with WSU’s unique and profession-oriented study abroad program.

More often than ever, WSU students ask: “Can I work in Europe after I graduate,” “What kinds of career opportunities exist there,” and “Where do I even begin to find answers?” WSU’s unique study abroad program, “Career Exploration Abroad,” is the first of its kind offered by any American university to offer an experience that answers those questions — and more.

Apply now!

Experience Business in Europe

Your Career Exploration Abroad program will host guest lectures and make site visits to a wide variety of organizations doing business in Europe, such as:

  • Government-run business development agencies
  • The Amazon Distribution Center
  • Cyber Fusion Center, a digital-solution company using ethical hacking practices
  • Anheuser-Busch InBev operations
  • National healthcare system
  • Medical tourism industry

Program Benefits

As part of WSU’s second Career Exploration Abroad cohort, you will fly to Prague, the capital of the Czech Republic, in May 2019 to spend three weeks earning six college credits learning about careers in Europe.

Thanks to WSU’s exclusive partnership with a successful study abroad organization, IFSA-Butler, you will live, study, and travel with other WSU students as well as professional career advisers.

You will take three courses, become familiar with Central European culture, and pursue professional development opportunities. You will also:

  • Meet executives representing for-profit and non-profit businesses and organizations
  • Network with Czech students from Prague College
  • Explore The Republic’s second-largest city Brno as well as Vienna, Austria

Global Leadership Certificate

Apply what you gain from your Career Exploration Abroad experience to requirements for this noteworthy achievement, which is recorded on your official WSU transcript. The certificate shows that you have cultural competency in your attitudes, knowledge, and skills, and that you are prepared to work with individuals, communities, and organizations that reflect diverse origins.

Career Exploration Abroad Boosts Your Career-readiness

The National Association of Colleges and Employers (NACE), of which WSU is a member, helps to define the skills that employers say students need to be ready for their careers. Several are learning outcomes built into your WSU undergraduate curriculum, such as critical thinking/problem solving, communications, diversity, and teamwork. NACE’s eighth identified skill, enhanced by your Career Exploration Abroad experience, is this:

“Global/Intercultural Fluency: Value, respect, and learn from diverse cultures, races, ages, genders, sexual orientations, and religions. The individual demonstrates openness, inclusiveness, sensitivity, and the ability to interact respectfully with all people and understand individuals’ differences.”
